Sleep apnea

Sleep apnea is a condition in which a person's breathing is repeatedly interrupted during sleep. These pauses can last from a few seconds to a minute and occur dozens, sometimes hundreds, of times per night, leading to a lack of oxygen in the body and poor sleep quality.


Causes of sleep apnea


There are several types of sleep apnea, each with its own causes:

  1. Obstructive sleep apnea (OSA) is the most common type, which occurs due to the collapse of the upper airway. This can be caused by:
    • Reduced muscle tone in the pharynx
    • Excess weight
    • Anatomical features, such as a wide and short neck
    • Enlarged tonsils, narrow airways, malocclusion
    • Alcohol consumption, smoking, or sleeping pills
  2. Central sleep apnea (CSA) this type is less common and is associated with disturbances in the functioning of the respiratory center of the brain. Causes include:
    • Neurological diseases, including brain stem infarcts Use of opioid analgesics
    • Chronic heart failure
    • Chronic kidney disease
  3. Mixed apnea a combination of both types.
